About Me

I was born at Baptist-Desoto in Southaven,MS, weighing in at 7lbs and 14oz! I wasn't a noisy infant nor a quiet infant, I just didn't bother anybody. When I was about 5 years old, my great grandmother actually held me back from fun activites and jovial moments with others because she was afraid that mosquitos would take my blood or something.

Even today, I never consider being in a group or group session with others because it's just how I was raised. I always worked alone and loved being independent and figuring out the problem on my own. I was always disciplined for laughing at something that my cousins and brother did and sometimes I just got little pops on the hand for something they did!

I'm really a quiet and silent person until I get around "some" family members. I get skeptical when it comes to explaining something and how it works. I struggle with processing information when someone asks me to do something because of a seizure disorder called Epilepsy. My brain actually slows down often, so I have to ask that person or people what did they said because it may not process the first time they say it or I might not do it correctly. I take medication for this, so thanks be to God, my seizures are under control. Without that medication, I wouldn't be sitting here today. I'm only following the doctor's and God's orders as instructed.

I'm VEERRRY shy and full of anxiety when standing in front of large and enormous crowds because I just feel more and more terrified and shaking like crazy. It's a possibility I might get over it, but I won't guarantee it! The worst thing for me is giving presentations an explaining those. It's a BIG communication problem. Not really that communicative with friends, just a little.

I also enjoy playing piano when I have free and useful time available. I'm trying to spend less time on the computer at home because things like this can be the main source(s) of seizures. I love playing keyboard because its just soothing to play something you like very much, and if you know the songs and the notes, it's even better. Guess what... I don't know the notes, but not to fret. There's also another way of playing and thats just playing by what you hear and transposing to the key you know or for "note-knowers", you can find the note instead.
